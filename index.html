艺术的故事0-1

发表于 2020-05-05

《艺术的故事》算是一本艺术史入门书，最早在顾爷的公众号看到，吸引我的是推文里面关于“视角”潮流变化的故事。拿到书粗翻了一下，里面有很多插图，序言里说是给小白入门看的，且尽量摒弃了繁复深奥的用语，比较符合我的身份。

这本书一共六百多页，分28个章节梳理了从史前到现代的艺术史发展脉络。写作顺序基本按照时间顺序，每个时间段内并列的介绍当时的主流艺术流派，我今天阅读的是导论和第一节，即史前起源。

导论这部分讨论了一个问题：什么是（好的）艺术？结论是大部分人喜欢真实自然的东西，或是符合成见的东西，认为真实的呈现就是好的艺术。但其实艺术更重要的是表达，即使是抽象的东西，如果真实的传达出了艺术家想要表现的，就有值得欣赏之处。作家用了很多对比，我印象最深刻的是一组卡拉瓦乔的圣马太像对比（15、16）。15是打破常规的作品，是画家对于一个小税务员突然被天使教写福音的画面的真实想象。马太地中海的发型，额前焦灼堆起的层层皱纹，裸露出来的粗壮小腿，笨拙蜷曲的手指，让这个形象充满了浓郁的人性感，因此在当时不被很多人接受，后来卡拉瓦乔只能又画了16，非常循规蹈矩，天使在天上飘着，马太头上顶着光环一副淡定又智慧的样子。但作者认为前一副更加“忠实而真挚”，因此在艺术性上甚于后者。

导论最后总结艺术是艺术家努力实现某种“和谐”，我理解的所谓和谐就是达成了对于想表达东西真实、准确的表达。这就带来一个问题，这样的和谐显见是主观的，是唯创作者心的，如果以此为标准，艺术好坏的评判也是主观的。然而，纵观历史，总有一些艺术家被奉为公认的大家，他们的作品在舆论中被认为是不同凡响的。这究竟是因为他们走运成名，因此一切作品都被吹捧呢？还是因为他们的艺术确实’优于‘他人的艺术？如果是后者，这就显示出艺术评价中的客观性（大众能够达成一致），那么这种客观性是如何形成的？

作者在章节末尾说，我们了解艺术史的意义就在于回答上一段末尾的问题。通过了解每一个
时代对于艺术的评价标准和其走向，我可能对那个问题的答案也会更明晰吧。

第一章讨论古代美洲的史前艺术，认为艺术的发源在于祭祀。人们通过绘制某种现实中存在的实体（或者制造雕塑，过程中可能有符号化），并对其进行一番操作比如祭祀和礼拜，从而间接操控超自然力量。比如我雕一个狼头并且崇拜它，我打猎的时候狼神就会保佑我。这部分记住了出自塔希提的“战神奥罗像”（24）。这个作品本来被用来说明如果想表现一个人那么只需要极度简化的符号化表达就够了。但我真的觉得这个草编的桩子上加上两个斜杠两个圈很难让我相信这是战神奥罗……我刚看的时候还以为是一个加了装饰的筐……另一个是两幅洞窟壁画（19、20），画的是很逼真的野牛和野马。作者将其归入祭祀礼拜的用途中，但我觉得这两幅画太写实了，完全没有抽象化。我觉得不能完全否定史前人民有无意识创造的能力。

写到这我突然想起了一个一直的疑问，就是毕加索画的简笔画和我画的简笔画到底本质上有什么区别，进一步说，如果有有人能完美复刻毕加索的画，那么复制版和原版的艺术价值是否有区别？这背后的问题是，艺术作品的价值是否完全来源于其形式？也许看完最后先锋艺术的部分我会有新的想法，目前先放在这。

最后吐槽一下，这书太重了，必须搁在腿上坐着阅读，躺着举的手酸而且时常怕书砸到脸。这也算变相培养良好阅读姿态了吧。

理论生态学综述-栖息地破碎与生物多样性

发表于 2020-01-22

Abstract

The decline of biodiversity has become one of the most important problem we are facing, and habitat fragmentation has been considered an essential threat for species. However, the accurate effect of habitat fragmentation on biodiversity is still controversial. In this report, I review on the current knowledge, and analyze the possible effects from both size with theoretical models and empirical cases. In the end, I discuss in general the significance of theoretical ecology in conservation biology

I. Current knowledge of habitat fragmentation and its effect

The world where we now live is developing at an incredible speed. Beyond this rapid development, however, the ecological problems we are facing also become more severe than ever. One of these problems that has drew a growing attention is the great and accelerating loss of biodiversity. Among the various threatens species are under, habitat destructions are usually stated as of most importance in conservation studies.
Habitat fragmentation was generally defined in a 2002 paper as “the discontinuity, resulting from a given set of mechanisms, in the spatial distribution of resources and conditions present in an area at a given scale that affects occupancy, reproduction, or survival in a particular species” . This term and the term “habitat loss” describe a couple of concepts that are frequently mentioned in relevant researches. The first elegant and general ecological theory discussing the effect of habitat change to biodiversity was offered by MacArthur and Wilson. In their book “The Theory of Island Biogeography”, they predicted that the smaller and more isolated (fragmented) a habitat is, the fewer species it will support . Though being one of the founding tenets of the field of conservation biology, the island biogeography later gave place to metapopulation theory, which focused on only one species and removed the persistent mainland habitat and emphasized the importance of dispersal  .

The two theories above both predict a negative effect of habitat loss and fragmentation on biodiversity. For habitat loss, this negative effect is universally accepted, but the effect of habitat fragmentation has long been under debate. For example, a following theory named metacommunity theory which takes the interaction of species into consideration shown that, a patchy environment can promote coexistence between predators and prey by providing refuges for the victim species .
Part of this controversy comes from the difficulties of separating the effect of habitat fragmentation from that of habitat loss, considering these two processes have a large possibility of co-occurrence. And there is also a general pattern of biological impoverishment of fragmented habitats compared with more intact ones. In addition, the distinct definition of terms and measurement of empirical cases make it hard to compare different theories and studies . On the other hands, these limits also provide space for different perspectives from theoretical and empirical analysis. We will discuss them in detail in the following parts.

II. Theoretical analysis: possible positive or negative effect of habitat fragmentation

Is the effect of habitat fragmentation positive or negative on biodiversity? This problem is summarized as the SLOSS problem, with “SLOSS” standing for “the single large or several small”. An article in 2002 constructed a theoretical model to address this problem .
The model is generally based on the metapopulation theory, considering the dynamics of a single species on spatially homogeneous patches. The total amount A0 of habitat available is fixed, ruling out the effect of habitat loss, and the number of patches n = A0 / A, where A means area of habitat patches. As is obvious, n is negatively correlated with A, so there should be either a single large patch, or multiple small patches. The author estimated which condition is the optimal one for biodiversity with two measurement, the expected time to metapopulation extinction, and the metapopulation capacity.
What the author found was that the habitat fragmentation in this model (represented by several small patches) can exhibited positive or negative effect under different conditions. The conditions are mainly defined by three parameters, , , and A0. The species parameter  is defined as the ratio of the extinction and colonization rate parameters,  = e/c. The patch area scaling factor  integrates the effect of patch area on metapopulation dynamics.
Based on a series of theoretical calculations, the meta population capacity can be approximately given by:

M=n1-A0  f(d)
This equation suggested that if  > 1, SL is the optimal strategy for maximizing M , whereas  < 1 would lead to the SS strategy. This means if the metapopulation capacity of a patch network grows with patch area faster than linearly, then the patch size should be of priority and vice versa.

This equation indicated that when  < 1, the SS strategy is optimal, while the condition can be much more complex when  > 1. The optimal strategy in this case falls between SS and SL strategy, and can be concluded as follow: The optimal size of habitat patches increases (and thus their number decreases) with increasing patch area scaling factor  and , but with decreasing A0.

Overall, the habitat fragmentation has both positive and negative effects on species persistence in this model. A high number of habitat patches (a more fragmented habitat) can help the system gain the advantages of risk spreading, but it also leads to smaller patches, which may result in smaller capacity. It depends on the characteristic of the species (how sensitive to patch size and how easy to disperse etc.) to decide which the better strategy is. Noted that in this model, only one species is considered, so species interactions are not taken into consideration, and the model may need further development to better fit the reality.

III. Empirical analysis: different effect of fragmentation and corridor in real cases

The empirical literatures about the effect of fragmentation are of huge size, and are across different scale, area and taxon. Many studies support a negative effect of smaller patch size and spatial discontinuity. For example, in a 2016 article, researchers found that in the Brazilian Atlantic Forest, larger fragments had more amphibian species, more integer communities and a larger diversity of reproductive modes than smaller ones. In addition, the values were higher for all diversity measures in continuous forest sites compared to fragments . However, there are also studies suggesting that the fragmentation may not matter to biodiversity. One research analyzed the species diversities of plants, tenebrionid beetles and carabid beetles in a highly fragmented Mediterranean scrub landscape. After decoupling the effect of degree of fragmentation from that of area loss, fragmentation by itself seems not to have influenced the number of species . In another research conducted in agricultural landscapes in Southern Judea Lowlands, Israel, spider diversity even increased as fragmentation increased . These complicated and diverse result may be due to the extrapolation of a small-scale mechanism to a large-scale pattern, or ignorance of other mechanisms, especially those acting at landscape scales, as suggested by later articles .

IV. Significance of theoretical ecology in conservation biology

The ecology in the real world is very complex, with numerous parameters, factors and biological processes getting involved. In this case, it’s hard to decide what the best conservation management is without the foundation of theoretical ecology. If we make decision based on intuition, the result can be helpless or even disastrous. For example, the removement of wolves from Yellowstone National Park eventually led to the broke down of the original ecosystem, which is counterintuitive at the beginning. Theoretical ecology can give us a more comprehensive understanding toward the environment and ecosystem, predict the possible balancing point of state transit, and help us to find the most effective way to manage and conserve our home planet.

野生灵长类中一个抗疟疾基因的演化

发表于 2019-12-24

Tung, J., Primus, A., Bouley, A. J., Severson, T. F., Alberts, S. C., & Wray, G. A. (2009). Evolution of a malaria resistance gene in wild primates. Nature. https://doi.org/10.1038/nature08149

摘要

本文探究了野生狒狒种群（yellow baboon, Papio cynocephalus）中疟原虫类似寄生虫（Hepatocystis）的感染情况。发现：

  • 疟疾相关基因（FY/DARC）顺式作用元件上的SNP与狒狒抗寄生能力相关。
  • 该基因的变异会影响野外个体的体内基因表达水平，这点通过体外实验得到验证
  • 该基因的SNP位点及附近区域经历了非中性演化

背景

  • Yellow baboon

    https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yellow_baboon

    • Yellow baboons inhabit savannas and light forests in eastern Africa, from Kenya and Tanzania to Zimbabwe and Botswana. They are diurnal, terrestrial, and live in complex, mixed-gender social groups of 8 to 200 individuals per troop.
    • When traveling as a group, males will lead, females and young stay safe in the middle, and less-dominant males bring up the rear. A baboon group’s hierarchy is such a serious matter, and some subspecies have developed interesting behaviors intended to avoid confrontation and retaliation. For example, males have frequently been documented using infants as a kind of “passport” for safe approach toward another male. One male will pick up the infant and hold it up as it nears the other male. This action often calms the other male and allows the first male to approach safely.

  • Hepatocystis

    https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hepatocystis

    • Hepatocystis is a genus of parasites transmitted by midges (摇蚊) of the genus Culicoides. Hosts include Old World primates, bats, hippopotamus and squirrels. This genus is not found in the New World.
    • In endemic areas, the incidence can range from 24 to 75% in nonhuman primates. Although infection with Hepatocystis does not normally cause fever it may cause anaemia（贫血） in the host.

    ……Hepatocystis parasites do not produce the cyclical fever spikes typical of malaria in humans, but do produce anaemia and visible merocyst （裂殖子胚） formation, followed by scarring on the liver.

  • FY

    https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yellow_baboon

    • Duffy antigen/chemokine receptor (DARC) = Fy glycoprotein (FY) = CD234 (Cluster of Differentiation 234), is a protein that in humans is encoded by the DARC gene.
    • The Duffy antigen is located on the surface of red blood cells, and is named after the patient in which it was discovered. The protein encoded by this gene is a glycosylated membrane protein and a non-specific receptor for several chemokines（趋化因子）. The protein is also the receptor for the human malarial parasites Plasmodium vivax, Plasmodium knowlesi and simian malarial parasite Plasmodium cynomolgi.[8] Polymorphisms in this gene are the basis of the Duffy blood group system.
    • The genetic basis for the Fy(a-b-) phenotype is a point mutation in the erythroid specific promoter (a T -> C mutation at position -33 in the GATA box).[11] This mutation occurs in the Fy-b allele and has been designated Fy-bEs (erythroid silent). 在人类中，T到C的变换会使基因沉默，进而使得携带者具有疟疾抗性。
    • Relationship with Malaria
      • On erythrocytes, the Duffy antigen acts as a receptor for invasion by the human malarial parasites P. vivax and P. knowlesi. This was first shown in 1980. Duffy negative individuals whose erythrocytes（红细胞） do not express the receptor are believed to be resistant to merozoite invasion[78] although P. vivax infection has been reported in Duffy negative children in Kenya, suggesting a role in resistance to disease, not infection.[78] 即在人类中，该基因的表达与疟疾侵染呈负相关。
      • This antigen may also play a role in erythrocyte invasion in the rodent malarial parasite P. yoelii. The epitope Fy6 is required for P. vivax invasion.[21]
  • Firefly luciferase

    https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Luciferase#Firefly_and_click_beetle

    • The luciferases of fireflies（萤火虫的荧光素酶）– of which there are over 2000 species – and of the other Elateroidea (click beetles and relatives in general) are diverse enough to be useful in molecular phylogeny.[1] In fireflies, the oxygen required is supplied through a tube in the abdomen called the abdominal trachea. One well-studied luciferase is that of the Photinini firefly Photinus pyralis, which has an optimum pH of 7.8.

  • Fixation Index, Fst

    https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/FST

  • The fixation index (FST) is a measure of population differentiation due to genetic structure. It is frequently estimated from genetic polymorphism data, such as single-nucleotide polymorphisms (SNP) or microsatellites.
  • The values range from 0 to 1. A zero value implies complete panmixis; that is, that the two populations are interbreeding freely. A value of one implies that all genetic variation is explained by the population structure, and that the two populations do not share any genetic diversity.

方法

在狒狒中，SNP与寄生虫侵染风险强弱有无相关性？

  • 血检（screening/sequencing DNA samples extracted from baboon blood）
    • 确定感染情况：61.9% 的感染率，随群体（social group）和采样时间而有波动，推测与家域范围及与载体（vector），一种摇蚊的接触有关
    • 对homologous FY cis-regulatory region测序：找到了6个SNP，其中一个A/G位点与感染显著相关，G alleles越多，感染风险越低

顺式调控元件的SNP是否通过影响基因表达来影响寄生虫侵染风险？

  • 基因表达水平检测：pyrosequencing（焦磷酸测序）
    • 在蛋白编码区找到了两个SNP位点作为marker标记不同allele
    • 在保留了RNA的101个狒狒里找出了38只上述marker杂合的
    • 测序并比较同一个体中不同cis-regulatory region调控的allele的表达水平，排除反式作用因子的作用，看allelic imbalance，发现有有区别的也有没区别的：暗示不止一个顺式作用元件变异影响表达？
    • 理论上，如果一个顺式作用元件有影响，那么它的纯合应该比杂合的imbalance水平低
      • 4/6个之前发现的SNP位点数据可以用来检测，发现其中一个C/T位点杂合的不平衡表达显著高于纯合，但只能解释22%，暗示不完全
      • Q：控制其他SNP相同？
      • 关注的A/G位点有37/38杂合，无法进行此类比较
  • 体外实验比较
    • 分别对A/G C/T位点建质粒，连接报告基因（firefly luciferase），在HEL cell line中表达。发现两个SNP都能显著提高基因表达水平（其他SNP？）与人类中的沉默表达不同

以上结果暗示FY的SNP可能通过两种方式影响寄生虫侵染

  • 作为受体的基因调控元件，改变寄生虫的direct access to baboon erythrocyte
  • 作为整体调控元件（？），通过影响个体的整体免疫水平，如改变相对白细胞数量

狒狒中的SNP位点是否与人类的T/C位点类似，经历了非中性演化（non-neutral evolution）？

  • 比较了1个FY连锁微卫星位点与35个中性位点的Fst，发现FY-linked microsatellite的Fst偏高

Insight

  • 独立起源的表型差异在不同灵长类物种中具有某种功能、遗传机制和演化历程中的趋同及平行性（parallelism)

    … In this case, not only are these similarities present on the molecular level or on the level of trait association, as shown by previous work, but they also extend to the mechanism that links molecular and phenotypic variation
