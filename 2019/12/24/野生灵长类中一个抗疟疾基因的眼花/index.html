野生灵长类中一个抗疟疾基因的演化

发表于 2019-12-24

Tung, J., Primus, A., Bouley, A. J., Severson, T. F., Alberts, S. C., & Wray, G. A. (2009). Evolution of a malaria resistance gene in wild primates. Nature. https://doi.org/10.1038/nature08149

摘要

本文探究了野生狒狒种群（yellow baboon, Papio cynocephalus）中疟原虫类似寄生虫（Hepatocystis）的感染情况。发现：

  • 疟疾相关基因（FY/DARC）顺式作用元件上的SNP与狒狒抗寄生能力相关。
  • 该基因的变异会影响野外个体的体内基因表达水平，这点通过体外实验得到验证
  • 该基因的SNP位点及附近区域经历了非中性演化

背景

  • Yellow baboon

    https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yellow_baboon

    • Yellow baboons inhabit savannas and light forests in eastern Africa, from Kenya and Tanzania to Zimbabwe and Botswana. They are diurnal, terrestrial, and live in complex, mixed-gender social groups of 8 to 200 individuals per troop.
    • When traveling as a group, males will lead, females and young stay safe in the middle, and less-dominant males bring up the rear. A baboon group’s hierarchy is such a serious matter, and some subspecies have developed interesting behaviors intended to avoid confrontation and retaliation. For example, males have frequently been documented using infants as a kind of “passport” for safe approach toward another male. One male will pick up the infant and hold it up as it nears the other male. This action often calms the other male and allows the first male to approach safely.

  • Hepatocystis

    https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hepatocystis

    • Hepatocystis is a genus of parasites transmitted by midges (摇蚊) of the genus Culicoides. Hosts include Old World primates, bats, hippopotamus and squirrels. This genus is not found in the New World.
    • In endemic areas, the incidence can range from 24 to 75% in nonhuman primates. Although infection with Hepatocystis does not normally cause fever it may cause anaemia（贫血） in the host.

    ……Hepatocystis parasites do not produce the cyclical fever spikes typical of malaria in humans, but do produce anaemia and visible merocyst （裂殖子胚） formation, followed by scarring on the liver.

  • FY

    https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yellow_baboon

    • Duffy antigen/chemokine receptor (DARC) = Fy glycoprotein (FY) = CD234 (Cluster of Differentiation 234), is a protein that in humans is encoded by the DARC gene.
    • The Duffy antigen is located on the surface of red blood cells, and is named after the patient in which it was discovered. The protein encoded by this gene is a glycosylated membrane protein and a non-specific receptor for several chemokines（趋化因子）. The protein is also the receptor for the human malarial parasites Plasmodium vivax, Plasmodium knowlesi and simian malarial parasite Plasmodium cynomolgi.[8] Polymorphisms in this gene are the basis of the Duffy blood group system.
    • The genetic basis for the Fy(a-b-) phenotype is a point mutation in the erythroid specific promoter (a T -> C mutation at position -33 in the GATA box).[11] This mutation occurs in the Fy-b allele and has been designated Fy-bEs (erythroid silent). 在人类中，T到C的变换会使基因沉默，进而使得携带者具有疟疾抗性。
    • Relationship with Malaria
      • On erythrocytes, the Duffy antigen acts as a receptor for invasion by the human malarial parasites P. vivax and P. knowlesi. This was first shown in 1980. Duffy negative individuals whose erythrocytes（红细胞） do not express the receptor are believed to be resistant to merozoite invasion[78] although P. vivax infection has been reported in Duffy negative children in Kenya, suggesting a role in resistance to disease, not infection.[78] 即在人类中，该基因的表达与疟疾侵染呈负相关。
      • This antigen may also play a role in erythrocyte invasion in the rodent malarial parasite P. yoelii. The epitope Fy6 is required for P. vivax invasion.[21]
  • Firefly luciferase

    • The luciferases of fireflies（萤火虫的荧光素酶）– of which there are over 2000 species – and of the other Elateroidea (click beetles and relatives in general) are diverse enough to be useful in molecular phylogeny.[1] In fireflies, the oxygen required is supplied through a tube in the abdomen called the abdominal trachea. One well-studied luciferase is that of the Photinini firefly Photinus pyralis, which has an optimum pH of 7.8.

  • Fixation Index, Fst

    https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/FST

  • The fixation index (FST) is a measure of population differentiation due to genetic structure. It is frequently estimated from genetic polymorphism data, such as single-nucleotide polymorphisms (SNP) or microsatellites.
  • The values range from 0 to 1. A zero value implies complete panmixis; that is, that the two populations are interbreeding freely. A value of one implies that all genetic variation is explained by the population structure, and that the two populations do not share any genetic diversity.

方法

在狒狒中，SNP与寄生虫侵染风险强弱有无相关性？

  • 血检（screening/sequencing DNA samples extracted from baboon blood）
    • 确定感染情况：61.9% 的感染率，随群体（social group）和采样时间而有波动，推测与家域范围及与载体（vector），一种摇蚊的接触有关
    • 对homologous FY cis-regulatory region测序：找到了6个SNP，其中一个A/G位点与感染显著相关，G alleles越多，感染风险越低

顺式调控元件的SNP是否通过影响基因表达来影响寄生虫侵染风险？

  • 基因表达水平检测：pyrosequencing（焦磷酸测序）
    • 在蛋白编码区找到了两个SNP位点作为marker标记不同allele
    • 在保留了RNA的101个狒狒里找出了38只上述marker杂合的
    • 测序并比较同一个体中不同cis-regulatory region调控的allele的表达水平，排除反式作用因子的作用，看allelic imbalance，发现有有区别的也有没区别的：暗示不止一个顺式作用元件变异影响表达？
    • 理论上，如果一个顺式作用元件有影响，那么它的纯合应该比杂合的imbalance水平低
      • 4/6个之前发现的SNP位点数据可以用来检测，发现其中一个C/T位点杂合的不平衡表达显著高于纯合，但只能解释22%，暗示不完全
      • Q：控制其他SNP相同？
      • 关注的A/G位点有37/38杂合，无法进行此类比较
  • 体外实验比较
    • 分别对A/G C/T位点建质粒，连接报告基因（firefly luciferase），在HEL cell line中表达。发现两个SNP都能显著提高基因表达水平（其他SNP？）与人类中的沉默表达不同

以上结果暗示FY的SNP可能通过两种方式影响寄生虫侵染

  • 作为受体的基因调控元件，改变寄生虫的direct access to baboon erythrocyte
  • 作为整体调控元件（？），通过影响个体的整体免疫水平，如改变相对白细胞数量

狒狒中的SNP位点是否与人类的T/C位点类似，经历了非中性演化（non-neutral evolution）？

  • 比较了1个FY连锁微卫星位点与35个中性位点的Fst，发现FY-linked microsatellite的Fst偏高

Insight

  • 独立起源的表型差异在不同灵长类物种中具有某种功能、遗传机制和演化历程中的趋同及平行性（parallelism)

    … In this case, not only are these similarities present on the molecular level or on the level of trait association, as shown by previous work, but they also extend to the mechanism that links molecular and phenotypic variation
